Research

I will start work at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory on characterization of detectors in early 2021.

I was on the TolTEC camera team, working on simulations for planning future surveys when the instrument is commissioned on the Large Millimeter Telescope in Mexico.

I have previously studied the hot gas X-ray emissions of giant ellipticals using the Chandra X-ray Observatory and XMM-Newton at the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics as an NSF REU fellow. In collaboration with the Chandra Galaxy Atlas team led by Dong-Woo Kim, I worked with Nazma Islam to test the pipeline for an XMM-Newton version of the atlas and analyzed the initial data products from this effort. Prior to my pursuit of astrophysics, I had a brief stint in optical physics at the Solid State Spectroscopy Laboratory at Kyoto University where I conducted the first pilot experiments exploring the properties of atomically-thin transition metal dichalcognides using terahertz microscopy.

Teaching

I am a current graduate student instructor for Astro 160 (Stellar Physics) at Berkeley. Previously, I was an undergraduate teaching assistant and grader for Physics 181, Physics 110, and Astro 105 at UMass.
